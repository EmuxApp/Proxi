@What is COVID-19?

                

                    @COVID-19 is a respiratory illness that can effect the lungs
                    and airways. It is caused by a virus called coronavirus and
                    is spread primarily through droplets generated when an
                    infected person coughs or sneezes, or through droplets of
                    saliva or discharge from the nose.
                

                
@What are the symptoms?

                

                    @COVID-19 has many possible symptoms. Below is a list of
                    many of them, with the topmost symptoms being the ones that
                    are most likely.
                

                
    
                    
  • @Fever
    • 
                    
  • @Dry and persistent cough
    • 
                    
  • @Shortness of breath
    • 
                    
  • @Muscle aches and pains
    • 
                    
  • @Sore throat
    • 
                    
  • @Headache
    • 
                    
  • @Runny or sniffy nose
    • 
                    
  • @Diarrhea

@What should I do if I feel unwell?

                

                    @If you feel unwell, visit your local medical centre's website for advice.
                

                
@I/a family member has experienced symptoms of COVID-19. How long do I have to stay inside for?

                

                    @Public Health England recommends the following steps in
                    regards to the duration of self-isolation:
                

                
    
                    
  • @If you live with others and you or one of them have symptoms of coronavirus, then all household members must stay at home and not leave the house for 14 days. The 14-day period starts from the day when the first person in the house became ill.
    • 
                    
  • @For anyone in the household who starts displaying symptoms, they need to stay at home for 7 days from when the symptoms appeared, regardless of what day they are on in the original 14-day isolation period.
    • 
                    
  • @PHE says that if you feel better after 7 days or self-isolating, you can return to your normal routine. If you have not shown signs of improvement, you should visit NHS 111 online, or by phone if you have no internet access.
    • 
                    
  • @A cough may persist for several weeks in some people, despite the coronavirus infection having cleared. A persistent cough alone does not meant hat you must continue to self-isolate for more than 7 days.
    • 
                

                
@Can I be tested for COVID-19?

                

                    @As of Wednesday 29th April, all over-65s and non-home
                    workers can now get tested, along with all essential
                    workers. This means that if you need to leave home to do
                    your job, you can now get tested, even if you are not an
                    essential worker by definition. You can also now get tested
                    if you live with an over-65 and you're exhibiting
                    symptoms. For more information on getting tested ─ and to
                    find out how to book a test, see the
                    Government guidelines.
